The Caesar Cypher

The Caesar Cipher, named after Julius Caesar, as it's told he used it to communicate with his generals, is a type of substitution cipher. This is where each letter in the 'plaintext' is changed to a letter that is a chosen distance down the alphabet. With a chosen shift of 1, the letter a would be replaced by the letter b, the letter b with c, and so on.


The Caesar Cipher offers little to no security against codebreakers. Being a 'Monoalphabetic' cipher, each letter is changed by the same rule as all of the others. So once the shift has been discovered, the cipher offers no further protection. This cipher would more likely be used by children to pass secret messages to one another, rather than used by anyone who is seriously concerned with the security of their communications.
